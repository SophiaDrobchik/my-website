Theoretical Astrophysics & Nonlinear Dynamics

My research lies at the intersection of theoretical astrophysics and applied mathematics, focusing on protoplanetary disks and the gravitational collapse processes that lead to star and planet formation. I am deeply fascinated by nonlinear dynamics, chaotic systems, plasma physics, and the role of symmetry in astrophysical phenomena.

Currently a third-year Astronomy student at Saint Petersburg State University under the supervision of Sergey Khaibrakhmanov, I am also beginning my journey as an educator, passionate about sharing the elegance of astronomy and mathematics.

My current research project, supervised by Sergey Khaibrakhmanov, investigates the stability and evolution of protoplanetary disks through analysis of the Toomre parameter. The work explores how gravitational instabilities may seed the formation of planets in early stellar systems. Read abstract

Teaching

Since 2024, I have been a teacher of astronomy at the Saint Petersburg Youth Mathematical School, where I design and deliver courses for motivated high school students passionate about space and mathematical thinking. Since 2022, I have also worked as a private tutor in mathematics, physics, and computer science, helping students develop deep conceptual understanding and problem-solving skills.

Teaching is not just a profession for me — it is a vocation. I am deeply committed to making complex ideas accessible and inspiring curiosity in my students. My dream is to one day lead my own university-level courses: a rigorous course in advanced mathematics and a special topics seminar in theoretical astrophysics, where I can share the beauty and power of these disciplines with the next generation of scientists.
